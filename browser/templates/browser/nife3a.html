Group 3a [NiFe]-hydrogenases

Properties

Group [NiFe] Group 3: Cofactor-coupled bidirectional [NiFe] hydrogenases
Subgroup [NiFe] Group 3a: F420-coupled
Function Directly couples oxidation of H2 to reduction of F420 during hydrogenotrophic methanogenesis. Reverse reaction proposed to occur during methylotrophic methanogenesis. Electrons flow between H2-activating hydrogenase subunits to F420 reductase subunit.
Activity Bidirectional
Oxygen Tolerance O2-tolerant
Localisation Cytosolic

Distribution

Ecosystem Distribution Methanogenic environments such as anoxic soils, marine sediments, and gastrointestinal tracts
Taxonomic Distribution Encoded in all hydrogenotrophic methanogens

Important Notes

This subgroup also includes a small lineage of enzymes in Thermococci. As this order does not synthesize F420, these hydrogenases presumably couple H2 oxidation to the reduction of another flavin or nicotinamide compound.
